Game Formats Explained

2 Man Best Ball (Fourball)

In this format, two golfers play as a team, with each player playing their own ball throughout the round. After each hole, the lower of the two scores is recorded as the team's official score for that hole. This format allows for a more aggressive style of play, as one player can take risks knowing their partner can still post a solid score.

Skins (Net & Gross)

A skins game is a match play format where each hole is worth a "skin." The player with the outright lowest score on a hole wins the skin. If there's a tie, the skin carries over to the next hole, increasing its value. This continues until a hole is won outright.

  • Gross Skins: The winner is determined by the actual score (gross score) without using handicaps. This rewards the best raw score on the hole.
  • Net Skins: Handicaps are applied to each player's score on a hole-by-hole basis. The winner is the player with the lowest net score, which levels the playing field for golfers of all abilities.

Net Medal (Stroke Play)

This is a classic stroke play format where every shot counts. Each player's final score is their "net" score, which is calculated by taking their total number of strokes for the round (gross score) and subtracting their full course handicap. The player with the lowest net score at the end of the round is the winner.
